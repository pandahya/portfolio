    research & concept development

    Since we were designing for a dystopian future, we did research on the topic of gene editing and designer babies, as well as construct the dystopian environment our app would exist in, to be able to better imagine how our app would exist. I took the lead in this research and was cognizant to look deeply into the ethics of the topic because of how new and controversial the technology is in the present; I didn't want the project to end up in a dangerous direction with negative implications.

    We first wanted to lay out and articulate how exactly our app would work (the features) as well as determine the target audience.

    moodboard + gestural design

    Each team member compiled a distinctive moodboard to start off the visual design direction and made corresponding gestural designs to imagine how the moodboards might translate to an interface.

    user flow chart

    We laid out a user flow chart of basic steps and actions we determined were important to the core concept and value of our app.

    hero flow

    From the user flow chart, we extracted a hero flow that would be the base of our wireframes.

    key screens

    From the hero flow we decided on 4 key screens representing the features of our app that we laid out at the start, and to further visualise each of our design directions. Starting with a base sketch for the layout of the UI elements, we each applied our design direction as we saw fit.

    user interviews & testing

    Even though our app exists in a dystopic future, we still wanted to interview people as close to our target audience as possible: parents of the present. We talked to aspiring parents, new parents, and experienced parents to get their persepcties on having/raising children. We also made sure to talk to people actively working with gene editing technologies/in a related field (who are also parents) and people who are religious to get their perspective of the technology.

    We also asked about our 3 design directions to understand what the target audience would feel more comfortable interacting with and which UI was able to best convey the concept.

    design system

    Taking the interview feedback into consideration, we finalzed the color palette and font system as per our consolidated design direction.
